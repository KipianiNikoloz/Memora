# Product Definition

## Vision

Memora turns personal memories into a structured library of growth. The product helps users capture meaningful experiences, reflect on what those experiences taught them, and revisit past moments with supportive AI guidance.

## Library Metaphor

- Books represent personal memories.
- Knowledge represents life experiences.
- Categories represent emotional tags.
- Life phases represent shelves.
- Past challenges represent archives.
- The full product experience represents a self-reflection moment.

The metaphor must be functional, not decorative. Browsing, filtering, saving, revisiting, and insight generation should all reinforce the sense that the user is building a library of their own life.

## Problem

Users often miss their own progress because achievements, challenges, and emotional growth are not tracked in a structured way. Social comparison can amplify negative self-talk, reduce motivation, and make personal growth feel invisible.

## Solution

Memora provides a digital personal library where users store meaningful life moments as entries. Each entry captures a significant moment or achievement, the user's emotional state, and a reflection on what the moment meant or taught them.

The product is not a random photo album, calendar, or generic journal. Its focus is growth, challenges, lessons, emotional patterns, and meaningful experiences.

## Value Proposition

Memora shifts users from social comparison toward self-comparison. It helps them recognize progress, transform life experiences into actionable knowledge, and receive gentle encouragement when revisiting the past.

## MVP Goals

- Let an individual user capture a meaningful memory quickly.
- Let a user tag emotion and life phase so the memory has structure.
- Let a user revisit entries through a library-style interface.
- Let the AI librarian produce supportive reflection in user-controlled tones.
- Preserve a calm, private, low-friction emotional experience.

## Non-Goals

- Clinical diagnosis, therapy, or medical advice.
- Public social feeds or comparison mechanics.
- Team analytics as an MVP default.
- Long-form productivity dashboard behavior.
- Automated life scoring or judgmental progress ratings.
