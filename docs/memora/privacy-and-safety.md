# Privacy and Safety

## Privacy Principles

Memora stores sensitive emotional data. The product must feel private, editable, and user-controlled.

Expected future behavior:

- Entries are private by default.
- Users control what they write, edit, and delete.
- Emotional labels and reflections are never treated as public content by default.
- Organizational use cases require explicit privacy and consent design before implementation.
- AI should receive only the context required for the requested action.

## Wellness Boundaries

Memora supports reflection and self-awareness. It must not present itself as clinical care.

The product should avoid:

- Diagnosis.
- Treatment plans.
- Medical or therapeutic claims.
- Crisis management authority.
- Scores that imply the user's life or emotional state is objectively good or bad.

## AI Safety Posture

AI responses should be gentle, bounded, and humble. They can say that a moment sounds difficult, meaningful, encouraging, or worth revisiting. They should not claim to know the user's mental state or future.

## Crisis-Like Input

If a user writes content suggesting immediate harm or crisis, future implementation should:

- Respond with care and urgency.
- Encourage contacting trusted people or local emergency services.
- Avoid long reflective analysis.
- Avoid presenting the AI as sufficient help.
- Preserve dignity and user agency.

## Trust Signals

The UI should make privacy visible through clear language, editable data controls, terms and privacy links, and settings that explain how memories and AI features are handled.
