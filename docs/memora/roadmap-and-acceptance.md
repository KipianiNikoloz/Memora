# Roadmap and Acceptance

## Hackathon MVP

- Public homepage with the message "Every life deserves a library."
- Email authentication.
- Private library for individual users.
- New entry creation with title, emotion, memory, lesson, tags, and life phase.
- Library explorer with shelves, search, and filters.
- Entry detail view for revisiting memories.
- AI librarian responses with Motivational, Humorous, and Wise tone modes.
- Basic insights for mood trends, milestone highlights, and revisit recommendations.
- Empty, loading, error, disabled, focus, and validation states.
- Clear privacy and wellness boundaries.

## Post-MVP

- Stronger pattern detection across emotional trends and life phases.
- Better revisit recommendation logic.
- Export and deletion controls.
- Wellness and productivity integrations.
- More customizable shelves and tags.
- Organization-oriented pilots only after individual privacy foundations are mature.

## Acceptance Checklist

A future implementation is aligned with this knowledge base when:

- Users can describe Memora as a library of personal growth after the first screen.
- Entry creation is fast and emotionally gentle.
- Library browsing supports shelves, filters, search, and revisit behavior.
- AI guidance is supportive, tone-controlled, and non-clinical.
- Insights help users notice progress without scoring or diagnosing them.
- Private emotional data is protected by default.
- Empty, loading, and error states use calm library language and clear actions.
- The visual system follows the parchment, burgundy, terracotta, gold, teal, Cormorant Garamond, and Inter direction.
- The product serves individual users first and keeps organizational use secondary.
